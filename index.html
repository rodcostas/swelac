Trade Data 2025
Sweden × LatAm: Trade Snapshot 2025
According to data obtained by SWELAC from Statistics Sweden (SCB), Sweden's trade with Central & South America reached SEK 40.8bn in exports and SEK 30.6bn in imports in 2025. Imports rose +12% and exports +2% compared to 2024. Largest export markets: Brazil (SEK 16.4bn), Mexico (SEK 9.7bn), Chile (SEK 4.1bn), Peru (SEK 2.2bn), Argentina (SEK 2.2bn). Largest import sources: Brazil (SEK 10.7bn), Guyana (SEK 9.1bn), Chile (SEK 4.2bn). SWELAC anticipates further growth across additional partner countries through 2026 and beyond.

Who We Are

The Business Link Between Sweden and Latin America

Sweden exports SEK 40.8 billion to Latin America annually. Imports are growing at +12% year on year. The corridor is active — and largely understructured. SWELAC exists to change that: to give companies, institutions, and decision-makers on both sides a place to meet, align, and build.

🎯
Our Mission
We are the business link between Sweden and Latin America. Since our inauguration at the Swedish Parliament alongside GRULAC and ambassadors from across the region, we have built that link through structured dialogues, signed agreements with governments, and direct access to decision-makers — not through declarations, but through work.
🔭
Our Vision
To be the foremost platform for trade, investment, and institutional partnership between Sweden and Latin America — expanding the bilateral relationship country by country, sector by sector. We have signed Letters of Intent with Ecuador and El Salvador. We are just getting started.
📊
What We Do
We convene round tables between Swedish and Latin American stakeholders. We bring together ministries, embassies, companies, and accelerators in the same room. We track bilateral trade data so our members understand where flows are moving. And we connect people who otherwise would not have met.
⚖️
How We Operate
With integrity, transparency, and a long-term view. We work across the triple helix of state, academia, and industry — on both sides of the Atlantic. We prioritize relationships that create durable value: for businesses, for institutions, and for the communities where trade actually lands.
